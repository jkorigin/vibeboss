# Contributing

Short version: **Vibeboss is read-only OSS at the moment. Fork freely; PRs aren't accepted yet.**

## Why read-only

Vibeboss is solo-maintained by a non-technical operator who's transparent about that bandwidth. Accepting external PRs would mean ongoing review, triage, communication, and quality-gatekeeping work that isn't realistic right now. Rather than open the door and respond slowly (or close PRs people put effort into), the posture is honest from day one: *the source is public so you can read, learn, fork, and adapt — but contributions aren't merged back.*

This is a common pattern for solo / personal OSS projects. It's not closed-source; it's just "open for reading, not open for collaboration."

## What you can do

- **Read it.** Everything is here. Read the [decision files](decisions/), the [CHANGELOG](CHANGELOG.md), the [design docs](docs/design/). The framework's reasoning is documented.
- **Fork it.** Apache 2.0 grants you the right to fork, modify, distribute, and build commercial layers on top. Go.
- **Adapt it.** Take patterns that work for your own AI-agent workflow. Mix and match. Vibeboss is conventions + hooks; nothing's load-bearing on Vibeboss itself.
- **Open issues for discussion.** If you find a real bug or want to flag something interesting, an issue is fine — just understand there's no commitment to action.

## What you can't do (right now)

- Open PRs expecting merge.
- Expect fast issue response.
- Expect a roadmap of community-requested features.

## When this changes

This boundary moves when one of three things happens:
1. A co-maintainer joins who's bandwidth-rich enough to absorb the review/triage load.
2. The maintainer's bandwidth changes (sabbatical, dedicated time, etc.).
3. There's enough demand that the framework's marginal contribution-burden becomes worth taking on.

When that happens, this file will be rewritten and the posture will open up. Until then, treat Vibeboss as a public artifact, not a community project.

## Forks are explicitly welcome

If you fork and ship something cool, drop the maintainer a note (via issue or whatever channel exists at the time) — interested in seeing what people do with this. No obligation either way.
